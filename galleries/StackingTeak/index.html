On one occasion in 2009 I organized work at a teak plantation. The task was to do a thinning and stack the wood in a safe place for further processing.

Two cants were wrapped in insulating material to prevent moisture from climbing up into the logs. Then logs got stack on top of that.

          Depending on the weight and the strength of the man it takes two for each log. We looked for the thickest logs first so that the most valuable material gets stored with priority.

                    It rained more and the area simply doesn't dry. You can see clearly how the water is standing between the trees. Walking in that area is difficult and even more so with a heavy load. One sinks in easily up to 30 cm and actually has to fight the mud. Not pretty ...

                          That's the stack after two days of work with 5 men. The count is 220 out of 1300.
