## **Client**: AXA Groupsolutions  
**Location**: Cologne, Germany  
**Duration**: 2015  

---

### **Objective**:  
Introduce **Acceptance Test-Driven Development (ATDD)** practices to project leaders and team members to improve collaboration, testing processes, and product quality.

---

### **Solution**:  

- **Workshops on ATDD**:  
   - Designed and conducted **introductory workshops** to teach project leaders and team members the fundamentals of **Acceptance Test-Driven Development (ATDD)**.  
   - Explained the ATDD process, focusing on collaborative requirements gathering, writing tests before implementation, and verifying outcomes.  
   - Demonstrated how ATDD fosters shared understanding among **developers, testers, and business stakeholders**.

- **Hands-On Training with Cucumber**:  
   - Provided practical training sessions using **Cucumber** to write acceptance tests in a readable, behavior-driven format.  
   - Introduced **Ruby** as the scripting language for creating executable acceptance tests.  
   - Delivered examples and exercises to help participants gain confidence in creating and automating ATDD scenarios.  

---

### **Outcome**:  
- Equipped project leaders and team members with a solid understanding of **Acceptance Test-Driven Development**.  
- Enabled teams to write **executable acceptance criteria** using Cucumber and Ruby.  
- Improved collaboration between stakeholders, developers, and testers by aligning requirements and testing practices.  
- Laid the groundwork for adopting ATDD to enhance product quality and reduce defects.

---

### **Technologies Used**:  
- **Testing Framework**: Cucumber  
- **Scripting Language**: Ruby  

---

### **Key Contributions**:  
- Designed and delivered **ATDD workshops** tailored to project leaders and team members.  
- Provided hands-on training on creating acceptance tests with **Cucumber** and **Ruby**.  
- Fostered understanding of ATDD as a collaborative process to align testing, development, and business requirements.
